Soedirman

General of the Indonesian Army

12 November 1945 - 29 January 1950

Early life

General Sudirman became a national hero who was famous for his guerrilla tactics against the invaders

Soedirman was born in Purbalingga, Dutch East Indies 24 January 1916. And he adopted adopted by his uncle due to financial reason

Sudirman was enrolled at a school for natives (hollandsch inlandsche school).
In his seventh year of school, Sudirman was transferred to junior high school run by 'Taman Siswa'.
In his eighth year, Sudirman transferred to Wirotomo Junior High School.
By age 19, Soedirman had become a practice teacher at Wirotomo.

Teaching

After graduating from Wirotomo, Sudirman spent a year at a Muhammadiyah teacher's college in Surakarta, but later dropped out due to a lack of funds.

In 1936 he returned to Cilacap to teach at a Muhammadiyah elementary school, having been trained by his teachers at Wirotomo. Within several years Sudirman had become headmaster despite not having a teacher's certificate.

During 1936 Soedirman also continued to serve as a member of the Muhammadiyah Youth Group. He also preached at the local mosque.
At the end of 1937, he was elected as Chair of the Banyumas District of the Muhammadiyah Youth Group.
He was later put in charge of Youth Group activities throughout Central Java.

Soedirman act as teacher on local citizens training of the safety procedures for dealing with air raid that initiated by Dutch colonial government on early WW II.

Military life

After a year as a representative at the Japanese regency council board, In early 1944 Soedirman was asked to join Pembela Tanah Air or PETA.

After four months of training at Bogor, Sudirman was put in charge as PETA (Defenders of the Homeland) commanders of the battalion stationed at Kroya, Banyumas, Central Java.

Sudirman and several of his fellow PETA soldiers formed a BKR branch in Banyumas in late August 1945 after proclamation of Indonesian independence

On 20 October 1945 Sudirman was put in command of the Fifth Division with the rank of colonel, after Java has divided into different military commands.
On 12 November 1945, at the first general meeting of Army leadership, Sudirman was elected Commander of the Armed Forces (TKR) and promoted to general.

He led The Battle of Ambarawa against British troops and the NICA from November to December 1945. Because he succeeded in obtaining victory in this battle, Soedirman was confirmed as commander of the Armed Forces on 18 December 1945.

On 5 October 1948 he was diagnosed with tuberculosis, which led to his right lung being collapsed in November 1948

Sudirman, along with a small group of soldiers and his personal doctor, traveled south and began a guerrilla resistance for seven months after Dutch assault on 19 December 1948.
Sudirman and his troops managed to escape from Dutch troops and making their headquarters at Sobo, near Mount Lawu.
There Sudirman commanded military activities throughout Java, including a show of force in Yogyakarta on 1 March 1949.

End of Life

In July 1949 Sudirman was recalled to Yogyakarta and forbidden to fight further. In late 1949 Sudirman's tuberculosis relapsed, and he retired to Magelang, where he died slightly more than a month after the Dutch recognised Indonesia's independence.

On 10 December 1964, he was declared a National Hero of Indonesia.
